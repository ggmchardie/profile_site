LIBRARIES & THEIR SIGNIFICANCE

Libraries play an integral role as equalisers within our society. If a library exists within a community, staffed with trained librarians, an individual’s access to our shared culture is not dictated by how much money we have but rather the ability of the library to provide the community with services and resources. We often forget that access to technology is a luxury that many people within our global community cannot afford. This makes information and education something that is out of reach for many of people.

THE FUTURE


With the rise of digital media, there has been a large shift in the way in which we relate to and access information. As the needs of our communities have changed we are beginning to seeing a shift from libraries being large storehouses of print material to information platforms – a small part of a large digital network, providing access to both print and digital material. However, this shift has created new problems for our modern libraries – the large cost of cataloguing vast amounts of digital and print material and providing access to patrons as a free service. Too often, library budgets are being cut to save other essential services greatly inhibiting their ability to stay relevant and provide quality access to information.
