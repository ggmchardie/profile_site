Libraries & their significance

Libraries play an integral role as equalisers within our society,
for if a library exists within a community, staffed with trained
librarians, an individual’s access to our shared culture is not
dictated by how much money we have but rather the ability of
the library to provide community access to it. We often forget that
access to technology is a luxury that many people within our global
community cannot afford. This therefore increases the gap in the level
and quality of information and education received by individual’s.

The future

With the rise of digital media, there has been a large shift in
the way in which we relate to and access information.
As the needs of our communities have changed we are beginning
to seeing a shift from libraries being large storehouses of print
material to information platforms – a small part of a large digital
network, providing access to both print and digital material.
However, this shift has created new problems for our modern libraries –
the large cost of cataloguing vast amounts of digital and print material
and providing access to patrons as a free service.
Too often, library budgets are being cut to save other essential
services greatly inhibiting their ability to stay relevant and
provide quality access to information.

Proposed services and resources

Students will be able to register for an account using their student email or identification card permitting them full access to the site. LibU’s proposed services are;

​
  1. A large catalogue of digital material in a range of formats available for remote access.
  2. Access to this online catalogue (written in RDA) can be requested by libraries and other institutions.
  3. A mobile app that offers the same services.
  4. Free access to a range of student services and resources. These include;
  • A referencing guide and tool
  • An assessment guide and tool
  • A note taking guide and tool
  • A plagiarism checker
  • Course guides and information
  • Ask a teacher or librarian
